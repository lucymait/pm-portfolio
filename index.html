Problem & Sprint Focus

Mid-career professionals lack clear, personalised insight into which skills to prioritise for their next role — leading to inefficient upskilling and stalled progression. Sprint focus: Skill Gap Analysis — the prerequisite insight before any learning or progress tracking can be meaningful. Primary user: mid-career professionals with 5–15 years of experience pursuing a more senior role.

User Research — Key Finding

"The priority skills screen felt overwhelming and alarmist — too many critical items at once. The '68% gap closure' framing was significantly more motivating than deficit-based scoring."

— Reuben, P1 interview participant. PM, 5 years experience, banking/fintech.

Research surfaced a critical trust gap: users won't act on skill recommendations they don't understand the basis of. This directly shaped the V2 addition of Skill Calibration — a 3-question self-assessment before the radar is revealed.

A/B Test Design — Course Enrolment Optimisation

Problem: 2% course enrolment rate vs. 40% target. Users view recommendations but don't start. A 4-arm test targeting the CTA mechanism:

ArmVariantHypothesis
AControl — passive "Start course" buttonBaseline ~2%
B"Start now / Schedule for tomorrow" dual CTAImplementation intention → +8–12pp
CSocial proof: "1,247 PMs started this week"Peer signal reduces hesitation → +5–10pp
DStreak-risk urgency + direct module deep linkLoss aversion + reduced friction → +10–15pp
